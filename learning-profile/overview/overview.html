GOAL

The goal is, to cut it short, get good at coding. What defines good?... What I determine to be "good" is not only being able to write clean and efficient code but also developing an inquisitive and progressive nature.🤔


Why is it important to develop a inquisitve nature?... Developing an inquisitive mind drives a person to learn things quickly. It focues their attention at the task at hand. Wether practicing in the real world or the imagination, the MIND 🧠 can't tell the difference. Enough of my rambling but that's the goal.

HoW DOES ONE "GET GOOD"?

In terms of syntax literacy... practice a lot. Ideally, the best way to memorize syntax is to practice writing it apposed to trying to memorize HTML/CSS cheat sheets...It's not like I did that and wasted a bunch of time... Look we make mistakes and we learn. Coming up with this website idea was the best idea I've generated in a while. It lets me practice my syntax literacy and come up with interesting design choices. I'd say I've done quite well. Deserving of a round of applause.👏👏👏


Syntax literacy I would say is the appetizer🍢. Logical thinking and reasoning would be the main course🍜 and there is a lot on the plate. So naturally I would have to take breaks to "digest". Then go right back in.
So how do I get good at this? As I go deeper into the rabbit🐰 hole of Front End & Back End the logical thinking and reasoning would come to me inevitably. NO AVOIDING IT. I get good through exposure, then tackle each challenge with a flexible and dynamic mind. Able to discover my personalised learning efficiency for given challenge,
BOOM🎇exponential learning growth . . . . . . . . . . sounds about right.
